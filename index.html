01 / AI Product Strategy

Teaching AI to think like a CNC programmer

RoleSenior Product Manager
CompanyCloudNC
DurationJul 2023 — Present
FocusAI Manufacturing Software

Shipped to production, now core to CloudNC's AI manufacturing offering.

CNC programming is a genuinely deep discipline — tolerances, tool paths, machine physics — which makes it a hard problem for AI to touch credibly. I lead product strategy for CloudNC's AI-powered manufacturing software, which helps CNC programmers automate complex machining workflows without asking them to trust a black box.

That means owning the product from discovery through delivery: working directly with machinists and programmers to understand where automation genuinely earns its place in their workflow, translating that into a roadmap, and defending the technical credibility of the product with an engineering team that will not tolerate a feature that doesn't understand the physics of the cut.

My background in CAD/CAM and industrial design isn't a nice-to-have here — it's what lets me have that conversation in the first place.

02 / Enterprise SaaS Roadmap & Delivery

Running five agile teams toward one coherent roadmap

RoleProduct Manager
CompanyCurve Dental
DurationJul 2019 — Nov 2021
FocusPractice Management SaaS

Five teams, one roadmap — shipped to thousands of practices.

As Product Manager at Curve Dental I held product owner responsibility across five project teams running in parallel, using data-driven analysis of product metrics to keep stakeholders aligned and to inform roadmap decisions rather than relying on the loudest voice in the room.

Over two years I coordinated the beta and general release of several major, business-critical projects at once: a roles and permissions overhaul, a rebuild of the billing system, and improvements to the practice's clinical charting tools — each shipping into software used every day by thousands of dental practices across North America.

The job was as much about sequencing and stakeholder trust as it was about the roadmap itself — planning and executing product vision and strategy in a system where a mistake in billing or permissions has immediate, real-world consequences for a practice.

03 / Design Leadership

Rebuilding perio charting from the clinic floor up

RoleUX Team Lead
CompanyCurve Dental
DurationMay 2017 — Jul 2019
FocusClinical Workflow Redesign

10× increase in daily clinical usage post-rebuild.

Perio charting — the process hygienists use to record gum health, measurement by measurement, during every patient exam — is one of the most demanding clinical workflows in dental software: fast-paced, precision-dependent, and unforgiving of friction. I led the team that took it apart and rebuilt it.

I led a team of UX designers and business analysts through the full cycle — conceptualising the redesign, validating it directly against real clinical workflows, and delivering it into production. Part of the job before that was building the team itself: recruiting designers and analysts and establishing a design culture and practice that didn't exist yet at Curve Dental.

This is the project that best shows the throughline into product management: the same discovery rigor and validation discipline, applied before I owned the roadmap outright.
